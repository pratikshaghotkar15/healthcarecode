What is Health Insurance Policy ?

Health insurance is a financial arrangement in which individuals or groups pay premiums to an insurance provider in exchange for coverage of medical expenses. It is designed to protect individuals and families from potentially high healthcare costs by providing financial support for various medical services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, medications, and preventive care. Health insurance policies vary in coverage, deductibles, and co-payments and can be obtained through employers, government programs, or private insurers.

Eligibility Criteria for the plan

Eligibility for a medical insurance policy is determined based on the following criteria: Age: Typically, the entry age for the insured ranges from 18 to 65 years, but it can extend to 70 years in some cases. Children aged 16 days to 18 years can also be covered, and there may be provisions for newborn and post-natal care. Tests: Some insurers may require favourable medical test results, including routine blood and urine tests, as well as tests related to major organs. Applicants below the age of 45 generally do not need to undergo medical tests. Medical Condition: Pre-existing health conditions must be disclosed during the application process. This disclosure may trigger a waiting period and can affect the calculation of the premium. Reasons for buying health insurance plan

  • Beating Medical Inflation: Medical costs are rising due to technological advancements and treatment quality. However, health insurance provides coverage that ensures affordable healthcare without worrying about increasing medical expenses.
  • Safeguarding Your Wealth: Paying for hospitalisation out of your savings can leave a significant financial burden. Health insurance protects your money and assets, requiring only a small premium per policy period.
  • Protecting Your Family: Ensure the well-being of your entire household by purchasing health insurance that covers every family member. Use the benefits to fulfil the medical needs of each individual.
  • Tax Benefits: Medical insurance premiums are tax-deductible under Section 80D, reducing your overall tax liability

Things to Consider Before Buying Health Insurance Plans in India

Choosing the right policy requires careful consideration in the vast landscape of health insurance options. Here are some major points to keep in mind:

  • Sufficient Coverage: Ensure the policy you choose provides adequate coverage for yourself and your entire family.
  • Significance of Individual Plans: While employer-provided group health insurance is common, reviewing individual plans and their terms is beneficial to make an informed decision.
  • Compensation: Add supplementary coverage such as hospital cash and critical illness benefits to compensate for non-payable losses.
  • Update Existing Conditions: Keep your health insurer informed about any existing medical conditions to ensure seamless coverage.
  • Understand Terms and Conditions: Familiarise yourself with the details of your health insurance, including deductibles, waiting periods, cumulative incentives, and pre-existing sickness provisions
  • Opt for High Sum Coverage: Given the rising medical costs, selecting a policy with a high sum coverage is advisable.
  • Early Purchase Advantage: Buy a health insurance plan at an early age to enjoy a higher level of coverage while keeping your premiums affordable.
